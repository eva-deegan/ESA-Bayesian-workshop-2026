# ESA-Bayesian-workshop-2026
This is the repo for "A Hands-on Introduction to Bayesian Modeling for Ecologists", a workshop offered at the 2026 Ecological Society of America Annual Meeting. We will convene 9 am – 4 pm on Sunday July 26th.

### Workshop description
Ecologists are often faced with analyzing spatially, temporally, or
hierarchically structured data. Additionally, many ecological problems require synthesis
of multiple sources and types of data, some of which may be incomplete (e.g., missing or
misaligned data) or arise from a wide range of sampling distributions. Bayesian and
hierarchical Bayesian methods are well-suited to analyzing such “messy” data. This full
day workshop will provide an overview of Bayesian and hierarchical Bayesian modeling
at a relatively introductory level, including basic probability, model building, and
numerical implementation (MCMC) concepts. We will provide a Jags (Bayesian software
package) demonstration using the jagsUI package in R; participants must be familiar with
the R programming language. During the afternoon, participants will have the
opportunity to develop and implement a Bayesian model based on a selection of
ecological problems and data that will be provided. Participants are expected to leave
with an understanding of the fundamentals of Bayesian modeling. We will provide
reference materials so participants can explore the topics in greater depth. These
materials should serve as a jumping-off point for those interested in employing the
methods in their own research, or for those who simply want to familiarize themselves
with the topic.
